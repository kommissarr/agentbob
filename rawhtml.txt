    </span>
      </p>

    </div>
  </div>
  <p>Malaysia's Prime Minister Najib Razak has expressed <a href="/news/2016-05-28/outrage-in-malaysia-as-government-backs-islamic-law/7456322" target="_self" title="">support for strict Islamic laws</a> as he seeks to consolidate support of ethnic Malay Muslims at a party meeting this week, as frustration over graft and the economy cloud his prospects for the next election. </p><p>Mr Najib has battled calls to resign over the past 18 months as a <a href="/news/2016-03-29/najib-razak-bank-accounts-triggered-money-laundering-alert/7280244" target="_self" title="">scandal at his pet project, 1Malaysia Development Berhad (1MDB),</a> drew the anger of the public, opponents and members of his own United Malay National Organisation (UMNO) ruling party. </p><p>A new opposition party, led by former prime minister Mahathir Mohamad and former deputy prime minister Muhyiddin Yassin — who Mr Najib sacked for questioning his handling of 1MDB — is  threatening to split the majority Malay vote that has given UMNO victory in every election since independence in 1957. </p><p>Ahead of the annual party meeting, Mr Najib said it was the responsibility of Muslims to support a plan by the rival Pan-Malaysian Islamic Party to push for the adoption of 'hudud', the Islamic penal code, that sets out punishments like amputation and stoning.</p><p>"We want to develop Islam," Mr Najib said in an interview with a pro-government broadcaster. </p><blockquote class="quote--pullquote"><p>"Non-Muslims must understand that this is not about 'hudud', but about empowering the sharia courts."</p></blockquote><p>With rising prices and poor economic prospects for next year, Mr Najib is expected to bank on ethnic and religious sentiment to woo majority Malay voters. </p><p>An election is due by 2018. </p><h2>UMNO meeting to focus on Malay, Islamic interests</h2><p>Mr Najib said his policy speech at this year's UMNO meeting would focus on the interests of Malays and Islam. </p><p>"This is my speech as UMNO President, so my main audience are UMNO members and the Malays and 'bumiputera'," he said, using a term that roughly translates as 'sons of the soil', and includes Malays but not members of the ethnic Chinese and Indian minorities.</p><blockquote class="quote--pullquote"><p>"It doesn't mean we don't care at all about the others, but this is an UMNO assembly." </p></blockquote><p>Mr Najib faced the biggest challenge to his leadership last year, after reports that hundreds of millions of dollars was misappropriated from state fund 1MDB, which he founded.</p><p>He acted swiftly to preserve his position, sacking critics in his administration <a href="/news/2016-01-26/corruption-probe-clears-malaysian-prime-minister-najib-razak/7115712" target="_self" title="">and closing a graft investigation.</a></p><p>Nevertheless, the scandal clouds prospects for an early election that Mr Najib could call to cement his position, with multiple international investigations going on and a suit related to <a href="/news/2016-09-02/minister-names-malaysian-pm-as-key-scandal-figure/7809500" target="_self" title="">the case filed by the US Department of Justice</a>.</p><p><strong>Reuters</strong></p>
