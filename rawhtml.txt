            <p>Gold bounced from its lowest close in 10 months as traders hedge against any potential surprise from the U.S. Federal Reserve&#x2019;s meeting where policy makers are expected to increase interest rates for the first time this year.</p><p>Over the past month, the metal has tumbled as traders price in the odds of a rate hike, reducing the appeal of assets that don&#x2019;t pay yields or dividend. Donald Trump&#x2019;s election to the U.S. presidency also helped spur the sell-off as his transition team plans infrastructure spending of as much as $1 trillion, bolstering the growth outlook and spurring the rally in U.S. equities to records.</p><p>Gold futures rose 0.4 percent to settle at $1,163.70 an ounce at 1:36 p.m. on the Comex in New York. The price closed at $1,159 on Tuesday, the lowest since Feb. 5. Traders are betting there&#x2019;s a 100 percent chance that the Fed will boost borrowing costs Wednesday afternoon, according to Fed funds futures rate.</p>

        <p>&#x201C;It&#x2019;s the typical Fed day uncertainty&#x201D; that&#x2019;s causing the slight uptick in prices, James McDonald, chief executive officer of Index Strategy Advisors, which manages $53 million, said in a telephone interview.</p><p>While investors will be watching for Fed Chair Janet Yellen&#x2019;s comments on future monetary policy, they shouldn&#x2019;t expect much given the uncertainty over Trump&#x2019;s policies,&#xA0;Robin Bhar, an analyst at Societe Generale SA, said by telephone. &#x201C;Yellen&#x2019;s hands are tied and will be until there&#x2019;s more policy certainty from the incoming president,&#x201D; he said.</p><figure data-type="image" data-id="307937254" data-image-type="chart" data-image-size="full" data-align="center" class="figure-expandable"><div class="image"><img src="https://assets.bwbx.io/images/users/iqjWHBFdfxIU/iaQhR58s166U/v2/-1x-1.png" srcset="https://assets.bwbx.io/images/users/iqjWHBFdfxIU/iaQhR58s166U/v2/-1x-1.png 1200w" sizes="(min-width:761px) and (max-width:1020px) 680px, (min-width:1021px) and (max-width:1280px) 640px, (min-width:1281px) 740px, 100vw" data-native-src="https://assets.bwbx.io/images/users/iqjWHBFdfxIU/iaQhR58s166U/v2/-1x-1.png"></div></figure><p>Gold has been battered this quarter, with only sugar showing worse returns among the raw materials on the Bloomberg Commodity Index. The S&amp;P 500 Index and Dow Jones Industrial Average&#xA0;stock benchmarks have been trading at all-time highs this month, reducing demand for the precious metal.</p><p>A measure of the dollar against its overseas peers is near the highest in more than a decade, making gold dearer for those holding other currencies.&#xA0;Holdings of the metal in exchange-traded funds fell for a 23rd day to 1,830.6 metric tons on Tuesday, the lowest since June, data compiled by Bloomberg show. That&#x2019;s the longest slide since May 2013.</p><p>In other metals news:</p><ul><li>Silver futures also advanced on the Comex in New York, while platinum and palladium gained on the New York Mercantile Exchange. </li></ul>
